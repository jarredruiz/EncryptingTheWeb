What's Encryption?

Encryption is the basis of Cybersecurity and what it means. Encryption dates back to the days of Roman empire where letters in the alphabet would be changed in order to create a cryptic message. Encryption is when you convert plain text that is understandable to most, into a format know as ciphertext. Chipertext is the text after it has been encrypted and is unreadable until it can be decrypted. Encrypting data is important to technologically advanced world we live in. Since most communication is done over the Internet, encrypting those messages ensures that it is kept private. Only the sender and reciever of the message should have access to the readable decrypted message. Soon, there will be a standard for encryption and Internet safety. Organizations around the world advocate issues like Data privacy and confidentiality. One in particular is Electronic Frontier Foundation(EFF), if you would like to learn more please click here.
